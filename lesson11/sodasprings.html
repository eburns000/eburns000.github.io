Old Faithful's Got Nothing on Us

When you think of geysers, the first one that comes to mind is probably Old Faithful, found in Yellowstone National Park. But did you know that Soda Springs is home to its very own geyser?

In 1937, a well drilling company, in an attempt to create a natural hot springs swimming pool, drilled down over 300 feet. To their surprise, they unleashed a geyser caused by carbon dioxide gas mixing with water in an underground chamber.

Today, the geyser is the only capped geyser in the world and is controlled by a timer, erupting every hour on the hour. So as your family makes its way to the trails this summer, don't forget to stop into the visitor center at our very own geyser.
