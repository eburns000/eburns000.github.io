Notes - Master Mobile UX


Mobile First User Experience

  • A primary reason for developing for the least powerful device first is so that you can focus on content and core functionality.
  • Multiple devices per household, multiple sizes
  • Whatever you are coding, do mobile first (Eric Schmidt)
  • Responsive Retrofitting != Mobile First
  • Start small and scale up – things tend to grow better
It’s really hard to design products by focus groups. A lot of times, people don’t know what they want until you shot it to them – Steve Jobs Immediate >> Relevant >> Frictionless Be there >> Be Useful >> Be Quick Make interactions smooth and seamless Reachability 75% rely on their thumbs Easier to reach things at the bottom of the device Speed Matters ½ second delay = 20% drop in traffic 2 second day = 4% drop in revenue (Bing) Reduce image size SVG/webfonts lower bandwith optimize and minify CSS and JS files GZIP files to eliminate unnecessary data Primary Controls Move main nav bar to bottom of screen to make more reachable Keep it simple, and fluid Hamburger menu – not intuitive = word “menu” with a border 20% more clicks than hamburger menu alone Mobile Inputs / Forms Make it easy to understand and use and focus on the important things first. Reduce cognitive load and make them think less. Display labels inside fields – use float labels (so label displays at the top of the field) Check boxes larger, easier to press Be careful about how many Paginate the form or eliminate non-required fields Enable auto-complete on forms Show passwords, not hide them, to make it easier for people to enter them, but allow users to hide the passwords if they want to. This was the suggestion – honestly, I would not like this on a site, and I don’t think I would apply this suggestion in anything I would develop. Enter Credit Card details Space out the numbers evenly consider putting cc numbers, expiration date, CID, and zip code on the same line, and auto-advance Just trying to make it easier for people to enter their payment information User the right keyboard – i.e., number pad for credit card entry Use a standard anchor tag with the telephone number in the attribute to make calls easier to make “End-users, not tech, shapes the market” – Matt Haig Mobile advertising $25 Billion to $150 Billion over 5 year period, 2013-2017 Still, ads are designed for desktops Pop-up and in-betweener ads – most detested methods of advertising on a mobile device Make ads small and simple Expand size of the ad when clicked, so they can see it if they want to, but keep it small Video ads result in higher conversion rates Stop using pop-ups and pop-overs Keep it simple and sleek Content first – make it unobtrusive as possible Keep it simple, keep it appropriate, and think outside the box
